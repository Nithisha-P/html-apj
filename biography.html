Biography of Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and a notable figure in the field of aerospace engineering. He was also the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and he passed away on July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India.

Here is a brief biography of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Early Life and Education

  • Dr. Kalam hailed from a modest background. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife.
  • He had a strong inclination towards learning from a young age and was particularly fascinated by mathematics and science.
  • After completing his school education, Kalam pursued a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

Career in Aerospace Engineering

  • Dr. Kalam's career in aerospace engineering began with his work at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • He played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs, notably the successful launch of SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle) and PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle).
  • Dr. Kalam's contributions to India's space endeavors earned him the moniker "Missile Man of India."

Role in India's Defense Programs:

  • He was instrumental in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV.
  • Dr. Kalam was the Chief Project Coordinator of India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Presidency

  • In 2002, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. He was known as the "People's President" for his approachable and down-to-earth nature.
  • During his presidency, he focused on addressing issues related to education, particularly in the areas of rural development and providing quality education to the youth.

Post-Presidential Activities

  • After his term as President, Dr. Kalam continued to be an active advocate for science, education, and social issues.
  • He remained deeply involved in various educational initiatives and interacted extensively with students and youth across the country.

Legacy

  • Dr. Kalam's life and work continue to inspire millions of people, especially the youth, in India and around the world.
  • He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which focused on the development of India.
  • His contributions to science and his vision for a developed India have left an indelible mark on the nation's history.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a beloved figure known for his humility, wisdom, and dedication to the progress of India. His life story is often cited as an example of what determination, hard work, and a passion for learning can achieve.
